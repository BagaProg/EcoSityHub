    Causes of poor ecology

    in our cities

    1

    Factories release huge amounts of harmful substances into the air and water, polluting the environment. This leads to health problems for people and destruction of ecosystems.

    Cities are filled with cars emitting carbon dioxide and toxins. This contributes to global warming, poor air quality, an increase in respiratory diseases, environmental degradation, and reduced overall public health.

    2

    3

    People throw away millions of plastic bottles and bags daily, polluting rivers, seas, and soil. This threatens wildlife, damages ecosystems, and worsens environmental conditions.







    Solving the environmental problems

    of our cities

    Cities should develop public transport, bike lanes, and electric vehicles. Using electric buses and subways reduces CO₂ emissions, improves air quality, and decreases traffic congestion.

    Industrial facilities must use modern filters and purification systems. This reduces the release of harmful substances into the air, lowering pollution and protecting the environment.

    The development of renewable energy sources reduces dependence on fossil fuels. Installing solar panels on rooftops provides clean energy, reduces CO₂ emissions and reduces the load on the grid.
